Zone G

Concept structure plan for an 18,000 ha site. The site is next to Etihad Rail Dry Port Terminal and proposed Passenger Terminal.

The master plan approach is to capitalize on these proposed developments and create a polycentric gateway city comprising of a business park, a residential center and a hospitality and recreation center.

Shaam

Conversion of existing farms and properties into residential neighborhood comprising of villas and residential commercial buildings, cluster and neighborhood facilities, and a wide boulevard leading to the waterfront promenade. Project area is 151 ha., with a yield of over 900 residential units. Project currently under detailed design stage.

Wadi Shaam

Master plan of 100 ha site next to mountain area. Used GIS capabilities to determine buildable areas up to 5% slope and not in conflict with built and owned parcels of land. Worked closely with hydrology engineers to determine best location of hydrology channels and detention ponds. Community facility analysis covered not only proposed master plan but the wider region of the existing area including Shaam project. Project currently under detailed design stage.

Al Hulailah

Master plan of a site next to a protected mangrove area. The concept is to create a connected neighborhood by means of a connected sikka network traversing the site longitudinally, and orienting all streets toward the sea and mangrove area to preserve the view and for better legibility.

Plan includes parks and recreational areas designed around water channels.

Academic City

Sketch study of one of the activity nodes that are distributed throughout the site. For the Academic City, the accommodation area is the social center and the Green Pedestrian Web connect it to the rest of the development. The Activity Nodes serve as destinations along the pedestrian web. They are a break from the monotony of the linear quality of the paths. These nodes are activity generators, they punctuate the network and become a dominant urban space.

✕

Academic City

The Academic City’s major land use component are the educational plots covering almost 60% of the site.

Students and staff accommodations in the form of multi-storey buildings, villas and townhouses, take up about 10% of the total site area. This creates a more balanced environment that responds to one of the development principles of creating an integrated learning and living environment.

The support facilities take up about 14%. These include the various activity nodes, the university core areas, pedestrian web, open spaces and utility plots.

The road network was efficiently laid out, taking less than 20% of the site. This is supported by a pedestrian web that ensures safe and efficient pedestrian movement throughout the site.

Al Maireed Old Area Concept

Plaza or mini-parks were also proposed for wider areas or intersections, thereby creating a "breathing space among the tightly-woven urban fabric.
